CONFUCIUS: THE SCHOLAR’S ARROW

Preface

Chen Bobby Kim Ling

As a digital artist who’s painted with light across international galleries, I’ve won awards for merging tradition with technology. But this project? It’s the work I was born to create.


I’m ethnic Chinese, raised between worlds. My grandmother whispered tales of Confucius like bedtime stories; my teachers preached Silicon Valley innovation. I craved a story where ancient wisdom didn’t gather dust — where ren (benevolence) and yi (justice) could duel with the urgency of a superhero saga. Where my heritage spoke to a global audience.


Enter AI.

This book was born from a radical experiment: what if I use artificial intelligence (such as Deepseek.com and DouBao.com for examples) to become my detailed writer?


I laid the foundation — characters, themes, backdrops, and the soul of Confucious’ rebellion. Then, like a master guiding a brush, I collaborated with algorithms to deepen the world: generating Wuxia and star-wars like action sequences in a parallel universe, historical textures, and philosophical dilemmas. The AI was my digital inkstone; I remained the hand holding the brush.


Likewise, where there is html programming needed, Human Intelligence (HI) is the objective driven force, and AI just puzzles to work.


Throughout the book, everything follow the above order, including artifacts and video generations. So far as image and video generation is concerned, credit is given to Doubao.com as most are from them but only for the AI generation part as end products may have gone through HI digital art processing to ensure consistency of characters’ looks and backgrounds.


Intended Audience

This book is intended for the Gen Z age group and older as story may imply (although not explicit) violence and contents not suitable for young children.


Why reveal this?

Because I want Gen Z readers to see technology not as a threat, but as a torchbearer. Just as Confucius turned ink into a weapon against tyranny, we can turn AI into a tool for empathy. This story demonstrates how:



>Human imagination leads (I crafted the heart of Confucius’ struggle)


>AI amplified (It visualized chariot races I could barely dream of)


>Wisdom wins(Confucian values anchor every byte


To Gen Z wary of robots “taking over”: Fear not. Machines have no conscience, no courage to defy tyrants. You do. Confucius’ journey proves that true power lies in the human spirit — whether facing a corrupt lord or a complex algorithm. Let this book remind you: Tools don’t define heroes; choices do.


his is more than a story. It’s a bridge between my Chinese roots and Western storytelling, between 2,500-year-old philosophy and tomorrow’s technology. A proof that when human creativity pilots AI, we don’t just tell tales — we ignite revolutions.


Grab your bow. The future is waiting.


Chen Bobby Kim Ling

Digital Artist | International Award Winner
