THE HIDDEN RAGE OF WUKONG

Unseen Fury, Dark Fantasy Retelling

Chapter 1: Stone and Fury

The mountain cracked with a sound like the sky splitting in two.


For centuries, it had stood silent—a jagged peak of unyielding rock, its surface weathered by wind and rain. The villagers below called it Huaguo Shan, the Flower-Fruit Mountain, though no flowers grew near its summit. They whispered that the stone was actually alive. That it breathed.


They were right.


**CRACK.**


The boulder shattered. From the rubble, a figure uncoiled, shaking off dust and fragments of rock. Golden fur glistened in the dawn light. Sharp eyes, the color of molten copper, blinked against the sun.


Sun Wukong had been born.


He did not know his name yet. He knew only sensation—the chill of the wind, the warmth of the light, the power thrumming in his limbs. He stretched, and the earth trembled.


A noise drew his attention. Below the cliff, a troop of monkeys screeched, pointing at him with trembling fingers.


"A demon!" one cried.


"A god!" another gasped.


Wukong tilted his head. Then, with a laugh, he leaped.


The ground shook when he landed. The monkeys scattered, all but the eldest, who stood his ground, gnarled hands gripping a staff of gnarled wood.


"Who—what—are you?" the old monkey demanded.


Wukong grinned, baring teeth like daggers.


"I am... me."
